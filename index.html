About Charlie's Life
What is this?

Charlie's Life is an implementation of a famous cellular automata called "Conways Game of Life". The game consists of a two-demensional grid of cells that can have one of two states: Alive or Dead. The state of any given cell can change each round (or 'generation') based on the states of the neighbouring cells.

There are only a few simple rules:

  • Underpopulation - A cell with less than 2 living neighbors will die
  • Survival - A live cell with 2-3 living neighbors will survive
  • Overpopulation- A cell with more than 3 live neighbors will die
  • Reproduction - A dead cell with exactly 3 neighbors will come to life
Game Modes

The game has two modes:

  • "Its a Wonderful Life" - A black and white implementation, for the purists.
  • "LiteBrite, Britelife" - Nostolgia and aesthetics are important here.

The basic rules are the same for both modes. The "LiteBrite, BriteLife" mode is similar to a common variation on Conway's Game of life called 'Immigration'. In LiteBrite, each cell is randomly assigned a color unless there is a single common color among its neighbors. In this case, the cell will take on that common color.
